Architecture is the manifestation of intrapersonal relationships into built spaces. More precisely, architecture is half design, half instinctual influence (i.e., what we want/need from our structures) and all based on the human way of life – which is based on interaction with others. Every space revolves around the way we do (or don’t) socialize with other living beings. While saying that architecture strictly serves only human populations is reductionist, the Earth itself has in fact become shaped to human need, so most of our architecture has followed suit. Perhaps that’s simply it – architecture is a barometer for the human(kind) condition.
